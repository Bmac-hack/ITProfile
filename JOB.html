Why does the job appeal to me

I like the idea of exploring a wide range of new skills to advance my career even further combining this with what I learn from RMIT to apply this in my IT career. Essentially I would learn how to harden networks, learn about the Linux side of it more which isn’t something I encounter every day in the workforce and then I would also be able to get into the realm of virtualization, and a good feel for MS Infrastructure (Group policy, domain roles, AD / DNS / DHCP) - more importantly it would give me a deeper mechanical understanding of how Linux/Windows operates than what I can employ now. 

 

Experience & Qualifications required for the job

  • Microsoft certification that covers how to correctly administer the domain and sub-level features would be ideal. How to manage your staff/users and the software in the environment. 
  • A certification that covers the desktop and server/cloud side would be largely beneficial as a senior support staff member. Having this certification gives you a good foundation of the tool-set that Microsoft would have you employ in the everyday life of a qualified IT administrator on the  side. So rather than being a click-next administrator this would provide a better understanding of what’s under the hood. As an example - you would be a very well certified IT staff member that knew how to get things done rather than a clever researcher. These two cannot be mutually exclusive.
  • VMware Certification would cover how to utilize correct virtualization techniques so things don’t go wrong as easy and there is a full understanding of how Windows/Linux/Other systems get implemented whether it’s for raw material boxes or cloud infrastructure. By having this certification you are using VMware to its capability and applying it securely. This would be extremely handy to have on top of the Microsoft certifications where any virtualization will be beneficial as the two can be combined for various applications. Eg deploying a windows server 2019 box on a cloud farm for hosting a service
  • Cisco CCNA would provide further tools I need to gain a very deep understanding of how everything connects to a network. Without this knowledge or understanding how everything ties together on the network, this facilitates better problem management and well-handled deployments where there is no guesswork involved. This would cover routing tables, DHCP, DNS, Vlan’s, infrastructure, Power over ethernet, wiring up fibre/ethernet cables, and even how to secure the network correctly & use the cisco console effectively without needing to refer to the documentation every 5 seconds.
